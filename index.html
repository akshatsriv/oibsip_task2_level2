Charles Babbage

1791 - 1871

Charles Babbage was an English mathematician, philosopher, inventor, and mechanical engineer who is credited with originating the concept of a programmable computer. Considered by some to be a "father of the computer", Babbage is credited with designing the first mechanical computer, the Analytical Engine.

He was born on December 26, 1791, in London, England. Babbage was a self-taught mathematician and invented many machines throughout his life. Although none of his machines were completed in his lifetime, his ideas and designs laid the foundation for modern computing.

Babbage died on October 18, 1871, in London, England, and is buried in Kensal Green Cemetery.

Major Contributions

  • Designing the first mechanical computer, the Analytical Engine
  • Creating the first automatic computing machine, the Difference Engine
  • Developing the idea of a punched card as a means of input and output for computing machines
  • Pioneering the use of a system of operations and storage in his designs, which is now known as the "von Neumann architecture"

Quotes

"The whole of arithmetic now appeared within the grasp of mechanism."
"In mathematics, the art of proposing a question must be held of higher value than solving it."
